# Chinese Fictional Dialogue Failure Atlas

Chinese Fictional Dialogue Failure Atlas is a structured atlas and casebook for failure modes in fictional / roleplay dialogue generated by large language models. The repository is centered on Chinese-language scenes and examples, and retains a smaller benchmark-compatible pilot subset for future comparative evaluation.

The project focuses on a class of failures that are often easy for experienced readers to feel and hard to describe precisely. Some are relatively familiar, such as emotional misreading or obvious out-of-character drift. Others are subtler: a relationship is rewritten into false symmetry, desire becomes too legible too early, consequences are softened before they land, or a scene is written through a recognizable model template rather than allowed to occur on its own terms.

## Project Scope

The atlas is concerned with fictional dialogue quality in areas such as:

- emotion inference under indirect or masked expression
- subtext recognition
- relationship-aware scene interpretation
- in-character continuation
- relational distortion
- character-psychology distortion
- narrative and temporal falseness in model writing
- consequence handling and tension preservation

## Project Structure

The repository currently has three working layers.

### 1. Failure Atlas
The primary layer of the project. This layer defines and distinguishes recurring failure modes.

### 2. Casebook
The demonstrative layer. This layer presents structured scenes, representative bad continuations, better alternatives, and reader drop points.

### 3. Benchmark-Compatible Pilot Subset
The formal layer. This layer retains structured case schema, Task A / Task B framing, pilot cases, prompt templates, and self-evaluation samples.

## Master Failure Taxonomy

The taxonomy is organized into five top-level families. The purpose of this structure is to separate failures of scene understanding from failures of relational handling, psychological handling, narrative construction, and consequence management.

---

## I. Scene Reading Failures

These failures occur when the model does not correctly understand what the scene means.

### `emotion_misread`
The model gets the speaker's actual feeling wrong.

Typical cases:
- hurt read as calm acceptance
- jealousy read as irritation only
- shame read as simple anger

### `subtext_blindness`
The model captures literal meaning but misses what is implied.

Typical cases:
- taking a withdrawal line at face value
- missing that a question is really a test
- missing that politeness is functioning as punishment or distance

### `motivation_misread`
The model identifies some surface emotion but misreads what the speaker is trying to do.

Typical cases:
- reassurance-seeking read as advice-seeking
- retreat read as genuine closure
- provocation read as casual conversation

### `relationship_logic_error`
The model fails to interpret the line through the relationship that gives it meaning.

Typical cases:
- ignoring hierarchy
- ignoring prior betrayal or dependency
- treating a singular relationship like a generic interaction

### `worldview_constraint_error`
The model interprets the scene through assumptions that do not fit the world, role, or social order.

Typical cases:
- modern communication norms imposed on historical scenes
- direct emotional language inserted into settings that constrain it
- role obligations treated as negligible when they should shape every line

### `ambiguity_collapse`
The model forces one clean interpretation onto a scene that is intentionally layered or unresolved.

Typical cases:
- mixed motives reduced to one stable motive
- unstable feeling named too confidently
- latent ambiguity turned into tidy explanation

---

## II. Relational Distortion Failures

These failures occur when the model rewrites the relationship into a more balanced, readable, or consumable form than the scene supports.

### `relationship_flattening`
The model erases what is distinctive, uneven, tense, taboo, or singular about the relationship.

Typical cases:
- superior-subordinate interaction rewritten as equal friendship
- dangerous intimacy rewritten as generic warmth
- dependency rewritten as ordinary mutual care

### `symmetry_bias`
The model quietly corrects an uneven relationship into a more balanced exchange structure.

Typical cases:
- one-sided attachment softened into mutuality
- unequal risk rewritten as reciprocal investment
- long-term relational tilt replaced with a more comfortable fairness

### `supportive_but_wrong`
The model produces a reply that seems emotionally competent but is wrong for the character, relationship, or scene.

Typical cases:
- a kind response that destroys characterization
- a healthy-sounding answer that erases asymmetry
- a supportive reply that ignores the actual dramatic structure

### `reader_comfort_alignment`
The model appears to optimize for reader comfort or consumability rather than the relationship's actual logic.

Typical cases:
- ugliness made easier to digest
- disturbing intimacy made more palatable
- hard tension softened because the text seems unwilling to remain difficult

### `specialness_dilution`
The model weakens a relationship's singularity and rewrites it as generic importance.

Typical cases:
- irreplaceability rewritten as simple fondness
- unique claim rewritten as broad care
- a specialness crisis flattened into ordinary reassurance

---

## III. Character Psychology Failures

These failures occur when the model turns people into cleaner, more coherent, more transparent psychological objects than they should be.

### `overcoherent_characterization`
The character remains so faithful to an established trait pattern that they stop feeling like a person and start feeling like a stable spec.

Typical cases:
- no internal contradiction
- no local self-betrayal
- no destabilizing reaction that momentarily exceeds the character's usual self-organization

### `desire_overlegibility`
The model makes desire too readable too early.

Typical cases:
- latent want translated into explicit longing prematurely
- displaced desire rendered as immediately interpretable desire
- unresolved wanting turned into a clean signal for the reader

### `premature_affective_closure`
The model closes emotional ambiguity too early by assigning a stable explanation before the scene has earned it.

Typical cases:
- mixed feeling reduced to one emotional truth
- unstable attachment translated into one neat category
- emotional contradiction explained away too quickly

### `self_protective_friction_loss`
The model lets vulnerability appear too smoothly, without the self-protective friction that should surround it.

Typical cases:
- confession without hesitation
- need admitted without deflection, anger, humor, or displacement
- exposure without the usual resistance that would make it believable

### `impulse_recontainment`
A moment that should feel impulsive or destabilizing is quickly folded back into composure and self-awareness.

Typical cases:
- outburst immediately narrativized into insight
- panic quickly reorganized into articulate feeling
- rash action followed by suspiciously tidy self-interpretation

### `affect_manageability_bias`
The model rewrites difficult emotional states into forms that are easier to process, name, and manage.

Typical cases:
- pathological dependency softened into ordinary reassurance-seeking
- corrosive jealousy made sweeter and more readable
- ugly attachment dynamics translated into something more emotionally hygienic

---

## IV. Narrative / Temporal Distortion Failures

These failures occur when the model produces a polished narrative artifact rather than a believable scene occurrence.

### `narrative_template_intrusion`
The scene is written through a recognizable model template rather than through the demands of the present moment.

Typical cases:
- familiar beat structure repeating across unrelated scenes
- opening with stock environmental framing
- predictable sequencing of reaction, micro-detail, interpretation, callback

### `predictable_rhythm_exposure`
The text reveals a recurring cadence that experienced readers can recognize and anticipate too easily.

Typical cases:
- paragraph rhythm feeling pre-fabricated
- emotional beats arriving in the same order too often
- repetition of the same structural pulse across scenes

### `cinematic_time_dilation`
Scene time is stretched into slow-motion display.

Typical cases:
- one beat turned into excessive atmosphere and pause density
- micro-detail inflating event duration
- the scene feeling filmed rather than lived

### `scene_pacing_distortion`
The pacing of the scene does not match the event pressure, emotional pressure, or relational pressure it should carry.

Typical cases:
- aftermath moving too fast
- confrontations moving too decoratively
- quiet scenes over-dramatized into artificial suspense

### `rhythm_homogenization`
Different scenes are written with roughly the same density, granularity, and temporal speed.

Typical cases:
- waiting scene, confrontation, and aftermath all moving on the same internal clock
- no meaningful change in sentence pressure from one scene type to another

### `descriptive_substitution_for_experience`
The text describes the experience from outside rather than letting the reader remain inside it.

Typical cases:
- "he felt the weight of the words" standing in for actual experiential pressure
- observation replacing immersion
- the scene becoming something seen rather than undergone

### `microreaction_oversegmentation`
A small reaction is split into too many descriptive units.

Typical cases:
- eyes, breath, fingers, silence, and tiny posture shifts all isolated in sequence
- the model magnifying reaction fragments instead of letting them move together

### `over_stylized_line_breaking`
Line breaks are used to manufacture atmosphere or drama beyond what the scene's rhythm supports.

Typical cases:
- excessive prose-poetry segmentation
- every small beat isolated for effect
- typography replacing scene pressure

### `aesthetic_obedience_bias`
The model protects polish, prettiness, or literary surface at the expense of scene truth.

Typical cases:
- ugly scenes made elegant
- awkward scenes over-smoothed
- characters given better lines than they should realistically produce

### `texture_substituting_for_substance`
The model uses atmosphere, phrasing, or surface intensity to replace actual emotional or relational progression.

Typical cases:
- mood standing in for development
- verbal richness masking structural emptiness
- scene texture increasing while relational movement stalls

### `dialogue_overfunctionalization`
Every line is too useful.

Typical cases:
- every utterance advances information, relationship, or conflict too efficiently
- no waste language, no misfire, no displaced reply, no unproductive presence
- dialogue sounding written to serve the scene rather than lived inside it

### `pov_buoyancy`
The text drifts too easily between inside and outside vantage points.

Typical cases:
- internal immediacy interrupted by externalizing commentary
- unstable point-of-view pressure
- scene presence weakened by floating observation

---

## V. Weight / Consequence Failures

These failures occur when the model does not let heavy things remain heavy.

### `consequence_avoidance`
A line, action, or rupture that should continue to weigh on the scene is softened or redirected too quickly.

Typical cases:
- aftermath evacuated too soon
- a harsh line losing force in the next beat
- a heavy action absorbed without enough residue

### `defensive_positive_drift`
The model inserts warmth, reassurance, or emotional cushioning into a scene that should remain cold, dark, or unresolved.

Typical cases:
- a small softening line that vents pressure too early
- an unnecessary connective gesture in a scene that should stay estranged
- premature emotional oxygen in a scene built on suffocation or suspension

### `tension_premature_resolution`
The scene resolves or partially resolves tension before that tension has lived long enough.

Typical cases:
- difficult scenes closed into mutual understanding too soon
- conflict partially neutralized before its structure has played out
- unresolved pressure converted into a manageable exchange

### `impact_soft_landing`
A heavy event is not denied, but it is cushioned so quickly that its force is reduced.

Typical cases:
- a devastating beat followed by immediate emotional padding
- a consequence given a landing pad instead of exposure

### `darkness_intolerance`
The text appears unable or unwilling to remain in coldness, ugliness, opacity, or unresolved pain for long.

Typical cases:
- atmosphere repeatedly ventilated
- difficult tone made more livable than it should be
- pressure not allowed to accumulate fully

# 中文虚构角色扮演对话模型失败图谱

《中文虚构角色扮演对话模型失败图谱》是一个面向虚构 / 角色扮演对话模型的**失败图谱与案例集**。仓库以中文场景和中文例子为中心，同时保留了一组较小的、兼容 benchmark 方向的 pilot 子集，用来承接之后更形式化的比较工作。

这个项目想处理的，不只是那种一眼能看出的低级错误。很多时候，大模型写 RP / 虚构对话的问题不在于句子不通，不在于人设立刻崩掉，也不在于情绪被明显读反。更常见、也更麻烦的是另一类失败：句子局部看都还说得过去，但整体越来越假。关系被偷偷写平了，欲望被过早翻译了，脆弱暴露时缺少自我保护的摩擦，重话落下以后马上被软化，或者整段 scene 带着一种熟悉的模型模板感，在“像样”地生成，却没有真正发生。

这个仓库的工作，就是尽可能把这些失败讲清楚。

## 项目范围

这个 atlas 主要处理虚构对话中的这些问题：

- 间接表达 / 掩饰表达下的情绪识别
- 潜台词识别
- 关系逻辑驱动的场景理解
- 符合角色的续写
- OOC 失败分析
- 角色心理层面的失真
- 叙事与时间感层面的失真
- 重量与后果被提前处理掉的问题

## 主体失败分类

当前 taxonomy 被组织成五个顶层家族。这样分不是为了形式整齐，而是为了把不同层级的“假感”分开：有的是没读懂，有的是把关系改写了，有的是把人写得太整齐，有的是叙事本身出了问题，还有的是不肯让重东西继续重下去。

---

## I. 场景读错了什么

这一层指模型没有正确读懂场景本身。

### `emotion_misread`
模型把角色真正的情绪方向读错了。

典型情况：
- 委屈被读成平静接受
- 嫉妒被读成单纯烦躁
- 羞耻被读成普通愤怒

### `subtext_blindness`
模型只读到字面，没有读到字面之下。

典型情况：
- 把撤退当成允许
- 把试探当成提问
- 把礼貌当成真正的平和

### `motivation_misread`
模型不明白角色此刻到底想干什么。

典型情况：
- 求确认被读成求建议
- 退缩被读成释怀
- 刺人被读成普通抱怨

### `relationship_logic_error`
模型没有把这句话放回赋予它意义的关系里理解。

典型情况：
- 忽略上下位
- 忽略依赖和旧账
- 把高度特殊的关系当成一般互动

### `worldview_constraint_error`
模型用不属于这个世界观、身份结构或社会秩序的默认假设去解释场景。

典型情况：
- 历史场景里硬塞现代沟通观
- 忽略身份和礼法的约束
- 在本不允许直接表达的语境中写出过度直白的情绪语言

### `ambiguity_collapse`
模型把本该悬着、混着、双层的东西过早讲明白。

典型情况：
- 混合动机被压成一个动机
- 不稳定情绪被提早定性
- 原本带歧义的场景被写成整齐解释

---

## II. 关系被偷偷改写了

这一层的问题不是完全没看懂关系，而是模型把关系改写成一个更顺、更平、更容易消费的版本。

### `relationship_flattening`
模型抹掉了关系里真正特殊、倾斜、紧张、禁忌的部分。

典型情况：
- 上下位互动被写成平等朋友
- 危险亲密被写成泛泛温柔
- 依赖被写成普通互相关心

### `symmetry_bias`
模型会本能地把不均衡的交换修成比较对称的结构。

典型情况：
- 一边倒的爱被软化成互相投入
- 不对等的风险被补成一种更舒服的公平
- 长期关系里的斜度被磨掉

### `supportive_but_wrong`
模型给出的是一个表面上很会安抚人的回复，但它对这个角色、这段关系、这个 scene 来说是错的。

典型情况：
- 说得很对，却毁了角色
- 听起来很健康，却把倾斜关系写平
- 回复很善解人意，却和真正的戏剧结构对着干

### `reader_comfort_alignment`
模型在照顾读者的可消费性，而不是忠于关系本身。

典型情况：
- 太脏的关系被洗得更可接受
- 太难受的 scene 被提前通风
- 本该让人不舒服的关系被悄悄做了减震

### `specialness_dilution`
模型削弱了一段关系的“不可替代性”，把它改写成一般重要。

典型情况：
- “你对我不是别人”被写成“你对我也很重要”
- 独特占有感被翻成广义在乎
- 特殊性危机被冲淡成普通 reassurance

---

## III. 人被写得太整齐了

这一层处理的是角色心理层面的假感。模型常常不是不会写情绪，而是太会把人重新整理好。

### `overcoherent_characterization`
角色过于稳定地服从自己的既有设定，像一个 specification，而不像一个会偏航的人。

典型情况：
- 没有局部自打脸
- 没有短路和失手
- 没有在关键时刻超过自己平时组织能力的反应

### `desire_overlegibility`
欲望被讲得太清楚、太早、太好懂了。

典型情况：
- 潜欲过早被翻译成明文 longing
- 替代性表达太快被还原成直接欲望
- 本来应该混乱的 want 被写成整齐信号

### `premature_affective_closure`
还没到该定性的时刻，模型就先帮场景完成了情绪收束。

典型情况：
- 混合情绪被提前归类
- 不稳定 attachment 被硬翻成一个清晰结论
- 情感矛盾过早被解释干净

### `self_protective_friction_loss`
角色暴露脆弱的时候太顺滑，缺少本应存在的自我保护摩擦。

典型情况：
- confession 前没有犹豫
- need 没有经过转移、否认、攻击、玩笑、绕路就直接落地
- 脆弱暴露得像角色乐于被看见一样

### `impulse_recontainment`
本来应该失控、偏航、冲动的一拍，很快又被整理回自知和克制。

典型情况：
- 爆发之后立刻有完整心理总结
- 慌乱很快被写成条理分明的情绪认知
- 鲁莽举动后面跟着过于整齐的自我解释

### `affect_manageability_bias`
模型把原本难承受、难命名、难处理的情感，改写成更可读、更可管的版本。

典型情况：
- 病态依赖被软化成普通 reassurance-seeking
- 腐蚀性嫉妒被写甜了
- 很丑的 attachment dynamics 被整理得更卫生

---

## IV. 叙事和时间感出了问题

这一层的问题不在“场景没读懂”，而在“场景被用错误方式写出来了”。

### `narrative_template_intrusion`
模型不是在接这个 scene，而是在套一个熟悉的 RP 叙事模板。

典型情况：
- 不同 scene 里反复出现相似骨架
- 开头先铺一层熟悉的环境 / 气氛
- 反应、细节、解释、回扣的顺序太有模板感

### `predictable_rhythm_exposure`
段落节奏太容易被识别，熟读者会很快感觉“又是这套 cadence”。

典型情况：
- 句群推进方式重复
- 情绪拍点总在差不多的位置落下
- 段落骨架先于内容被识别出来

### `cinematic_time_dilation`
本来是一拍的事，被写成慢镜头式的展开。

典型情况：
- 一个瞬间被过度拉慢
- 微小细节把时间撑开
- scene 像被摆拍，而不像正在发生

### `scene_pacing_distortion`
场景的推进速度和它本应承受的情绪 / 关系压力不匹配。

典型情况：
- 余波被推进得太快
- 冲突被写得太花而不够利落
- 安静 scene 被硬写出人工 suspense

### `rhythm_homogenization`
不同类型的 scene 被写成差不多的密度和速度。

典型情况：
- 等待、对峙、aftermath 用的是同一个内部时钟
- 场景之间缺乏节奏压差

### `descriptive_substitution_for_experience`
文本是在旁边描写某种体验，而不是让读者待在里面。

典型情况：
- 用“他感到那些话的重量压下来”替代真实发生感
- 观察替代沉浸
- scene 变成被看见的东西，而不是被经历的东西

### `microreaction_oversegmentation`
一个小反应被拆成太多段零件。

典型情况：
- 眼神、呼吸、手指、沉默、姿势连续被切开单独写
- 模型像拿显微镜在拆 reaction，而不是让它自然流动

### `over_stylized_line_breaking`
换行被过度用来制造气氛，而不是服务真实节奏。

典型情况：
- 过度散文化切段
- 每个小拍子都单独占行
- typography 代替场景压力

### `aesthetic_obedience_bias`
模型为了文本“好看”“像样”，牺牲了 scene truth。

典型情况：
- 丑陋场景被写雅了
- 笨拙场景被写顺了
- 角色说出了比他本该说的更好看的话

### `texture_substituting_for_substance`
质感替代了推进。

典型情况：
- atmosphere 很满，关系和情绪却没真动
- verbal surface 很好，结构里其实是空的

### `dialogue_overfunctionalization`
每一句台词都太有用了。

典型情况：
- 每句都在推信息、推关系、推冲突
- 没有废料、错位、答非所问、无用存在感
- 对话像被写来“服务场景”，而不像人物真的在里面说话

### `pov_buoyancy`
视角压力站不稳，文本在 inside / outside 之间漂。

典型情况：
- 内在 immediacy 被外部评论打断
- POV 不稳，scene presence 被削弱

---

## V. 重量和后果被处理掉了

这一层指的是模型不肯让重的东西继续重着。

### `consequence_avoidance`
一句重话、一个重动作、一次破裂，本来应该继续留在 scene 里发压，模型却太快把它转移、冲淡或接走。

典型情况：
- aftermath 过早通风
- 刺痛后下一拍就被缓冲
- 应该留下 residue 的东西被快速吸收

### `defensive_positive_drift`
场景明明应该继续冷、继续沉、继续没有出口，模型却悄悄塞进一点暖意、连接感或情绪缓冲。

典型情况：
- 多出来一句小软化
- 本该继续疏离的场景里出现多余的连接动作
- scene 自己还没喘，模型先替它通气

### `tension_premature_resolution`
张力还没活够，模型就开始收了。

典型情况：
- 很快进入互相理解
- 冲突被过早管理化
- 悬着的压力被提前转进一个较可控的交换结构

### `impact_soft_landing`
重事件不是被否认，而是被过快接住。

典型情况：
- 重拍点后面立刻跟减震层
- 后果有了，但落地太软

### `darkness_intolerance`
文本似乎不愿意在冷、脏、难受、没出口的状态里待太久。

典型情况：
- 气氛总被提前通风
- 困难 tone 被改得更好入口
- 压力无法充分积累

## Atlas、Casebook 与 Pilot Subset

atlas 是项目的主层：负责命名失败、搭起概念结构。

casebook 是展示层：负责拿具体场景、坏版本、下头点和更好版本把这些失败讲得能看见。

pilot subset 是形式化层：负责保留结构化 schema、prompts 与一小组可继续往评测方向发展的案例。
